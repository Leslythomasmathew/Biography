Biography

The journey from Madeira to the pinnacle of global sports.

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro was born on February 5, 1985, on the small island of Madeira, Portugal. Growing up in a working-class neighborhood, his passion for football was evident from a very early age. He spent hours playing on the streets, developing the signature flair, speed, and agility that would later define his professional style.

He began his professional career with Sporting CP in Lisbon before catching the eye of Sir Alex Ferguson. Joining Manchester United in 2003 as a teenager, he gained international recognition, winning his first Ballon d'Or and Champions League title. His subsequent transfers to Real Madrid, Juventus, and Al Nassr cemented his status as a legendary goal scorer and leader.

Career Highlights

A spectacular club journey across Europe and Asia.

Sporting CP 1997 - 2003

The Foundation of a Star

Ronaldo joined Sporting CP's youth academy at age 12. Having progressed through all of the club's youth teams, he became the first player to play for the under-16, under-17, under-18, B-team, and the first team, all within a single season.

  • Promoted to first team at age 16.
  • Caught the attention of Sir Alex Ferguson in a 3-1 pre-season win against Manchester United.
  • Left a lasting impression on United players who urged Ferguson to sign him.

Manchester United 2003 - 2009

Entering the Global Stage

Signed as Manchester United's first-ever Portuguese player, Ronaldo inherited the legendary number 7 shirt. Under the mentorship of Sir Alex Ferguson, he transformed from a skillful winger into a clinical goalscorer, winning every domestic and European honor available.

  • Won 3 Premier League titles & 1 UEFA Champions League.
  • Awarded his first Ballon d'Or in 2008.
  • Scored 118 goals across all competitions.

World Class Player

Real Madrid 2009 - 2018

The Legendary Peak

Ronaldo joined Real Madrid for a world-record transfer fee. He became the club's all-time top goalscorer, establishing a historic rivalry with Lionel Messi and leading Real Madrid to a legendary three consecutive UEFA Champions League titles.

  • Won 4 UEFA Champions League titles & 2 La Liga titles.
  • Won 4 additional Ballon d'Or awards.
  • Scored a mind-blowing 450 goals in 438 official appearances.
RMCF

Juventus 2018 - 2021

Conquering Italy

Seeking a new challenge, Ronaldo completed a transfer to Juventus. He maintained his incredible goalscoring standard, helping the club secure league dominance and setting records in a third major European league.

  • Won 2 Serie A titles and 1 Coppa Italia.
  • Reached 100 goals faster than any player in Juventus history.
  • Named Serie A Footballer of the Year twice.

Al Nassr 2023 - Present

Pioneering a New Era

Joining Al Nassr in Saudi Arabia, Ronaldo made history with the most lucrative contract in football. He popularized the league globally and finished 2023 as the world's top goalscorer for the calendar year.

  • Led Al Nassr to the Arab Club Champions Cup in 2023.
  • Scored his landmark 900th professional career goal.
  • Catalyzed an influx of global stars to the Saudi Pro League.
